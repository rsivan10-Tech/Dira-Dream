# Israeli Residential Construction Methods

## Typical Structural System

Israeli residential buildings (binyanim) predominantly use a **concrete frame + block infill** system:

### Primary Structure
- **Reinforced concrete frame**: Columns + beams + slabs
- **Flat slab system**: Most common in modern construction — no beams, columns directly support slabs
- **Shear walls**: Concrete walls providing lateral stability against earthquakes and wind
  - Typically at stairwell/elevator cores
  - Sometimes at building perimeter
  - Full height, full width — CANNOT be removed

### Secondary Structure (Infill)
- **Concrete block walls (בלוקים)**: 10–20cm thick hollow concrete blocks
  - 10cm blocks: interior partitions (non-structural)
  - 15cm blocks: some interior walls
  - 20cm blocks: exterior walls (with insulation)
- **Autoclaved Aerated Concrete (Ytong)**: Lightweight blocks, increasingly common
  - Better thermal/acoustic insulation
  - 10–20cm widths available

### Mamad (Safe Room) Construction
- **Poured reinforced concrete**: NOT block infill
- **Walls**: 20–30cm thick reinforced concrete with dense rebar
- **Ceiling**: Part of the structural slab, or additional reinforced slab
- **Door frame**: Heavy steel frame cast into the concrete
- **Window** (if present): Small blast-proof window with steel shutter
- **Construction sequence**: Built as part of the structural frame pour, NOT added later
- **Cannot be modified**: Drilling into mamad walls compromises blast protection

## Wall Types in Israeli Plans

### Exterior Walls (~20-25cm)
```
| Plaster (1-2cm) | Block (20cm) | Insulation (4-5cm) | Plaster (1-2cm) |
```
- Total: 25-30cm including finishes
- ALWAYS structural in terms of building envelope
- Require permit to modify (window/door changes)

### Interior Partition Walls (~10-12cm)
```
| Plaster (1-2cm) | Block (10cm) | Plaster (1-2cm) |
```
- Total: 12-15cm including finishes
- Non-structural — CAN be moved/removed during shinuyim
- Cheapest modification: 15K-40K ILS for removal

### Structural Interior Walls (~15-20cm)
```
| Plaster (1-2cm) | Block/Concrete (15-20cm) | Plaster (1-2cm) |
```
- May be load-bearing or shear walls
- Require structural engineer assessment before modification
- If concrete (not block): likely shear wall — VERY difficult to modify

### Mamad Walls (~25-30cm)
```
| Plaster (1-2cm) | Reinforced Concrete (25cm) | Plaster (1-2cm) |
```
- THICKEST walls in the plan
- Distinctive in PDF: thickest line weight
- NEVER modifiable under any circumstances

## Wet Areas (Plumbing Considerations)

### Bathroom Construction
- Waterproof membrane under floor tiles
- Floor lowered 5-10cm for drainage slope
- Plumbing in floor slab or wall chases
- Moving bathroom = moving drain point = expensive (40K-100K ILS)
- Drain connects to building's main stack (vertical pipe)

### Kitchen Construction
- Water supply + drain for sink
- Gas line for stove (in some buildings)
- Ventilation duct for hood
- Kitchen moves are expensive due to plumbing + gas + ventilation

### Plumbing Stacks
- Vertical pipes running through all floors
- Located in wet walls (thicker walls near bathrooms/kitchens)
- CANNOT be moved — fixtures must connect to nearest stack
- Horizontal drain runs limited to ~3m from stack (gravity drainage)

## Common Israeli Construction Patterns

### Floor Plans
- Buildings typically have 2-4 apartments per floor
- Mirror-image apartments common (saves on plumbing stacks)
- Mamad rooms stacked vertically (structural continuity)
- Stairwell/elevator core in center of building

### Floor Heights
- Floor-to-floor: 3.0m standard
- Ceiling height: 2.60m (after slab + finishes)
- Slab thickness: 25-30cm
- Floor finish: 5-10cm (screed + tile)

### Balcony Construction
- Cantilevered concrete slab or supported by columns
- Railing: 105cm minimum height
- Typically not insulated
- Service balcony: smaller, for laundry/AC units
- Enclosing balcony requires building permit (changes envelope)

## Earthquake Standards (Teken 413)

Israel is in an active seismic zone (Dead Sea Transform fault):
- All buildings must meet seismic design standards
- Shear walls critical for lateral stability
- Cannot remove shear walls without engineering redesign
- Modern buildings (post-2005) have better seismic design
- Older buildings may require Tama 38 seismic reinforcement
